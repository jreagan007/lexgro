### Quick Answer (Featured Snippet Target - 60 words)
Fractional CMO costs for law firms typically range from $5,000-$15,000 per month, depending on hours, scope, and experience level. Most law firm engagements fall in the $7,000-$10,000/month range for 10-15 hours of strategic leadership. This is 60-75% less than a full-time CMO salary while delivering the same strategic value.

### Section 2: What Affects Fractional CMO Pricing (400-500 words)
**H2: 7 Factors That Affect Your Cost**

1. **Hours Required**
   - 10 hours/month vs. 20 hours/month
   - Project-based vs. ongoing

2. **Scope of Work**
   - Strategy only vs. strategy + execution oversight
   - Number of marketing channels

3. **Experience Level**
   - General marketing vs. legal industry specialized
   - Years of CMO experience

4. **Geographic Market**
   - NYC/LA vs. smaller markets
   - Remote vs. on-site requirements

5. **Firm Complexity**
   - Single practice area vs. multiple
   - Single location vs. multiple

6. **Current State of Marketing**
   - Starting from scratch vs. optimizing existing
   - Team in place vs. building team

7. **Engagement Length**
   - Month-to-month vs. 6-12 month commitment
   - Discounts for longer terms

### Section 3: Pricing Models Explained (300-400 words)
**H2: How Fractional CMOs Structure Their Fees**

**Monthly Retainer (Most Common)**
- Fixed monthly fee for defined scope
- Pros: Predictable, aligned interests
- Cons: May pay for unused hours
- Typical: $5,000-$15,000/month

**Hourly Rate**
- Pay only for time used
- Pros: Flexibility
- Cons: Can add up quickly, less commitment
- Typical: $200-$500/hour

**Project-Based**
- Fixed fee for specific deliverable
- Pros: Clear scope, defined end
- Cons: Not ongoing strategic support
- Typical: $10,000-$50,000 per project

**Hybrid Models**
- Base retainer + project fees
- Performance bonuses
